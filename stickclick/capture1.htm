'Screen Grabs'.

How to make screen 'captures' of PC programs to use in documents such as Word or PowerPoint.

The 'Print Screen' method.

It is very easy to insert an image of what's on your PC screen into a Word document.

Try this:

  1. Open up any application, e.g. Netscape.
  2. Press the 'Print Screen' (or 'Print Scrn') button on your keyboard (just to the right of the F12 key).
  3. Open up Word.
  4. From the Word menu, choose: 'Edit' > 'Paste'.
An image of your screen should appear in your document.
Page Layout view is best for viewing documents with images: (from the Word menu select 'View' > 'Page Layout').
  • The whole of the screen image will be included.
  • If the program was not 'maximized' i.e. taking up the whole of the screen, the image will include any other background programs or your desktop as shown below.


To make a screen capture of just the application window that you are using, use 'Alt + Print Screen'.

Try this:

  1. Open up the application you want an image of.
  2. Make sure it's window is not maximixed.
  3. Resize the window the way you want it.
  4. Hold down the 'Alt' key and press the 'Print Screen' button on your keyboard.
  5. Open up Word.
  6. From the Word menu, choose: 'Edit' > 'Paste'.
  • Only the image of the window you were working with will be included.


  • You can use exactly the same method to make up PowerPoint slides.
