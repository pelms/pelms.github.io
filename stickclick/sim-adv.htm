  Message Groups.

Message groups are a way of grouping messages in a folder according to their contents, and can be an effective way of getting some order from the chaos that your Inbox (or mine anyway) soon becomes.
Highlight a folder (e.g. the 'Inbox' folder) and switch them on from the menu:
    Folder > View Message Groups
(or right-click the inbox and chose 'View Message Groups')
 
You are given two message groups to start with: 'Main' and 'Deleted'.
(If you delete a message and it just disappears rather than having a big red cross put through it, then the 'Deleted' group is where you'll find it)
 
To create a new Message group, highlight the Inbox (by clicking on it once), then...
    File > New > Message Group...
(or right-click the inbox and chose New > Message Group...)
then fill out the form as you wish. Messages can be grouped by the contents of any (or all) of the header fields (e.g. To, From, Subject, cc).
 
e.g. to group all the mail from LMT Admin, fill out the form as shown...
 
 
 
 
 Or to group all mail with the word 'virus' in the subject heading, fill out the form like this...

 
You can also create Message Groups in your Outbox or in any other folder. They can be switched on or off, folder by folder, by highlighting the folder, clicking on the 'Info' button and then ticking or unticking the 'View Message Groups' box.
 
If a message fits the criterion for more than one message group (e.g. a message from LMT Admin, called 'Viruses' in the example above), it will be placed in the uppermost group on the screen. You can move the position of groups relative to each other by dragging them up or down with the mouse.

To delete a folder, simply highlight it (click on it once), and then click the 'Delete' button. You are then given the choice of either deleting all the messages in the group, or just the group itself (leaving the messages intact).
 

  Sharing Folders.

 Any of your folders in the Post Office (as opposed to Local Folders) can be shared with other people. For instance you could make one or more folders from your Post Office available to other members of your team.

To do this you need to know their User names (the ones with hyphens in e.g. 'an-other')

  • Highlight the folder you want to share
  • Click on 
  • Click on the 'Sharing...' button
  • You will be presented with the 'Access Permissions' form...

in which you need to put the user names of the people or groups you want to share the folder with.

e.g. If you have two user names: 'p-elms' and 'lib-sys'. Enter p-elms in the User / Group form and click 'Add' then lib-sys and click 'Add' again, to add the names to the list.

  • Highlight the first name in the list (click on it) and check the boxes depending on whether you want them to just be able to 'Read' what you put in, or to 'Write' to the folder, i.e. be able to add some of their own messages.
  • Clicking 'Admin' will let them add other people to the sharing list and change the settings.
  • When you have finished and clicked 'OK' and 'OK', the people on the list will have a folder added to their Post Office called after your user name. This will contain the shared folder.

  Sharing Address Books.

 Address books can also be shared with other people (as long as they are not 'Local' books).
  • Highlight the address book you want to share.
  • Click the  button to get the 'Book Properties' form.
  • Make sure 'Search in Compose' is checked, so that the book can be searched while composing a message.
  • Click the 'Sharing...' button to get the 'Access Permissions' form as before.
  • Fill out the same way as for 'sharing folders'.

When finished, the sharers will have new address book (prefaced with your user name) in their Address Book Organiser, which will give them access to any lists or addresses inside.
N.B When naming an address BOOK make sure there are no spaces in the book name.
e.g. 'My_Book' rather than 'My Book'.
 

  Simeon Options Settings.

 Get to the Options settings from the menu:

     Tools > Options > Edit...

 

Click on an icon from the scrolling left hand panel to give you the relevant options.
 

Suggested settings

  • User
    • Change this to alter the 'Signature' that appears at the end of all messages that you send.
  • Message Organiser.
    • Cross the box marked 'Enable Priority Headers' so that mail sent to you marked 'Urgent' will show up in red.
    • To make a message 'High Priority' or ask for a 'Read receipt', click the   button while you are composing a message.
  • Compose.
    • Cross the 'Options' boxes as shown, so that you don't ask for a read receipt for all mail you send.
  • Editor.
    • Fill in the 'Launch URL...' section as shown to let you go direct to a quoted web page by double clicking on it in an email (e.g. http://www.esys.ca/  for the Simeon site).

  Tips.

  • Right mouse-click on almost anything to bring up a shortcut menu of useful commands (e.g. right clicking on a message in your Inbox, will let you mark it as unread, to make it more obvious).
  • Change the sorting order of messages by highlighting a folder, clicking the   button and changing the 'Sort by:' option.
  • By default Simeon checks for new mail every 10 minutes. You can check manually, from the menu:
        Folder > Check New Mail  (while the inbox is selected)
  • When sending mail to a large number of people who are not on one of the Address book lists, paste the addresses into the BCC (Blind Carbon Copy) field rather than the TO field. This way, when the mail is printed, it doesn't include the long list of recipients (you will have to put at least one name into the TO field for this to work)
