How to . . . Change the resolution of computer monitors

(eg. making more of Unicorn fit on your screen)

  • In Windows Program Manager you should have a Program Group called either 'VGA Utility' or 'VGA Display'.
  • If you open this there should be an icon called either 'WinMode Utility' or simply 'WinMode'.
  •   If you double-click on this you should get something similar to this screen:
 
  • The recommendation is to check the '800x600' mark in 'Resolution'.
  • If you have the '640x480' mark checked, then you won't fit as much on your screen, whether it is Unicorn or MS Word, but it will be bigger.
  • If you check the '800x600' mark you will you will fit more on screen as it will be smaller.
  • If you have trouble reading the text on screen because it is too small, then setting the resolution to '640x480 may help.
 
N.B. While you are there, have a look at the 'Monitor Refresh Rates'.
The Refresh Rate determines how flickery the screen appears to the eye. The higher, the better. If you only have a refresh rate of 60Hz or less it can be very tiring on the eyes.

If you have a 15" monitor (as opposed to the older 14" monitors as on the student OPACs) then you can set the refresh rate to 75 Hz and get a flicker free picture. If you have an older monitor (only likely if you have a 486 PC rather than the newer Pentiums) then you should leave the refresh rate at 60Hz.
