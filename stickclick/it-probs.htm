Essential Information.

If you are reporting problems with IT equipment we need all of the following information:

  • Your name.
  • Your campus.
  • The serial no. of the PC or printer.
  • The model no. of the PC or printer.
  • The location of the PC or printer.
  • Any error messages.
  • Your contact number.

Full information is necessary - if the IT Support team does not have a solution to a problem, the fault report is passed to UWE IT Services. If we have all the required information it speeds up this process.

Response to faults from ITS.

ITS have published fault response targets:

Urgent faults.

Urgent faults are classified as Category One and are considered to be those which effect more than one person e.g. Unicorn crashing or network failure.

ITS have a target on Category One jobs of responding to them within 4 hours (this does not necessary mean a fix but they will contact us about the problem). They aim to fix the problem within 5 working days. Usually, however, fixes are much quicker than this.

Non-urgent faults.

Non-urgent faults are classified as Category Two and include those which, though they may be a nuisance for you, may just require you to move to another PC or use another printer.

The ITS target on Category Two jobs is an initial response to be received within 3 working days and for the problem to be fixed within 15 working days.

If problems have not been fixed within these times then inform your IT Co-ordinator or contact the IT Support team. We keep a record of all faults logged and will chase on your behalf.
